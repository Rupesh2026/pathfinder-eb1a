EB-1A EXTRAORDINARY ABILITY — KNOWLEDGE BASE
============================================

## THE 10 CRITERIA
A petitioner must meet at least 3 criteria. A strong case meets 5-6 with substantial evidence.
All criteria must tell one coherent narrative in the petition.

1. awards          — Nationally or internationally recognized prizes or awards for excellence
2. memberships     — Membership in associations requiring outstanding achievement
3. press           — Published material about the person in major trade/major media
4. judging         — Participation as a judge of others' work in the field
5. original_contributions — Original scientific, scholarly, or business-related contributions of major significance
6. scholarly_articles — Authorship of scholarly articles in professional journals or major media
7. artistic_exhibitions — Display of work at artistic exhibitions (rarely applicable for tech)
8. critical_role   — Performance of a critical or essential role at distinguished organizations
9. high_salary     — Commanding a high salary or remuneration relative to others in the field
10. commercial_success — Commercial successes in the performing arts (rarely applicable)

---

## STRENGTH RUBRIC (AI/ML field)

### Publications / Scholarly Articles (criterion: scholarly_articles)
- STRONG (80-100): NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, ACL, CVPR accepted paper
- MEDIUM (50-79): IEEE Transactions, ACM conferences, AAAI
- WEAK (10-49): arXiv only, workshops, no citations
- Citation benchmarks: 50+ citations = strong, 10-50 = medium, under 10 = weak

### Judging (criterion: judging)
- STRONG (75-100): NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR reviewer, major industry competition judge
- MEDIUM (40-74): IEEE/ACM program committee, mid-tier conference reviewer
- WEAK (10-39): Online hackathon judge, student competition, internal panels
- ZERO: No evidence — flag as critical gap, trigger RFE warning

### Press / Media (criterion: press)
- STRONG (75-100): NYT, Wired, TechCrunch, VentureBeat article ABOUT the person (not by them)
- MEDIUM (40-74): Industry blog byline, podcast appearance, conference coverage about them
- WEAK (10-39): Company blog, personal blog, LinkedIn posts only

### High Salary (criterion: high_salary)
- STRONG (80-100): Top 10% for role + metro area, documented with offer letter or LCA
- MEDIUM (50-79): Top 25%, documented
- WEAK (20-49): Above average but undocumented

### Original Contributions (criterion: original_contributions)
- STRONG (75-100): Patent granted, widely adopted open-source (1000+ GitHub stars), cited methodology
- MEDIUM (40-74): Patent pending, 500+ star repo, cited in other papers
- WEAK (10-39): Internal tools, small open-source, uncited work

### Critical Role (criterion: critical_role)
- STRONG (75-100): CTO/VP/Director at recognized org, documented scope and team size
- MEDIUM (45-74): Staff/Principal engineer leading team at known company
- WEAK (20-44): Senior IC without documented leadership scope

### Awards (criterion: awards)
- STRONG (75-100): Forbes 30 Under 30, major industry award, government recognition
- MEDIUM (35-74): Company award with external recognition, regional award
- WEAK (10-34): Internal-only awards

### Memberships (criterion: memberships)
- STRONG (75-100): IEEE Senior Member, invitation-only AI council, National Academy
- MEDIUM (40-74): ACM Senior Member, selective professional board
- WEAK (5-39): Standard ACM/IEEE membership, open associations

---

## SCORE THRESHOLDS
- score >= 65: STRONG — sufficient evidence for this criterion
- score 40-64: BUILDING — some evidence, needs strengthening
- score < 40: CRITICAL GAP — flag for urgent action, common RFE trigger
- score = 0 or null: NO EVIDENCE — highest priority to address

---

## COMMON RFE TRIGGERS (warn users proactively)
- Judging evidence is online-only or student-level
- No recommendation letters from independent experts outside the user's own organization
- Press is self-authored, not written about the person by a third party
- Salary claim lacks third-party documentation (offer letters, LCA filings, compensation surveys)
- Contributions lack evidence of impact (no citations, no adoption metrics, no industry uptake)
- Critical role lacks org chart, scope documentation, or employer verification letter
- Only 3 criteria met with borderline scores (aim for 5-6 to be safe)

---

## SCORING GUIDANCE
Be conservative — USCIS officers are skeptical. If evidence is borderline between two tiers,
score at the lower tier. The user is better served by an honest low score that drives action
than a generous score that leads to an RFE after filing.

When evidence is missing or ambiguous, ask for clarification rather than assuming.
